Content Scaling System
The Weekly Content Engine
Film 4–5 pieces of content in one 2-hour shoot block per week. One scriptwriter , one on-
camera talent, one editor . Target weekly content production cost: under ₹ 15,000 at scale.
MondayScript writing + shot list prep (2 hours). Review last week's top performer and identify
the variable that made it work.
TuesdayShoot day — film 4–5 Reels + photoshoot content in a single session. Batch
everything.
WednesdayEdit + caption writing + scheduling all posts for the week ahead. Post within 7:30–9
PM IST  window .
ThursdayStories + "Scent W ars" poll live. Monitor first-hour engagement on all posts. Reply to
every comment within 60 minutes.
FridayPerformance review: top hook, top CT A, top format. Document findings. Adjust next
week's scripts accordingly .

The Concept Pivot

The Philosophy: Beyond Decoration
From "Inherited Beauty" to "Generational Grit"
We are rewriting the definition of an heirloom. Traditionally, jewelry has been marketed as a way to amplify the passive beauty of the wearer. A spotlight on a woman who sits still to be admired.
The Shaya will shift this narrative. Our pieces are not just a tradition of beauty passed down through families; they are a tradition of determination, valor, grace, courage, and resilience.
The Shift: We are moving from the "Passive Queen" (who preserves the legacy) to the "Active Builder" (who creates it).

The Function: The jewelry is not a delicate object to be protected; it is a companion in the arena. It is the golden armor worn by the woman who is sweating, working, and sculpting her reality in real-time.

SLICKTEK: BRAND TONALITY & DIGITAL APPEARANCE GUIDE
1. BRAND CORE IDENTITY
The Persona: The "Steve Jobs of the Coffee Shop." Sophisticated, highly competent, slightly obsessive about details, and effortlessly cool.
The Core Emotion: Empowerment through precision.
The Vibe: Minimalist, engineered, tactile, and premium.

2. BRAND VOICE & TONALITY
SlickTek’s voice is confident, concise, and sensory-driven. We do not yell; we state facts with style.
Tone Pillars:
Engineered Precision: Speak about the products as if they are high-performance vehicles or luxury watches. Use technical terms accurately but accessibly.
Instead of: "Our stand is really strong."
Say: "Precision-machined from aerospace aluminum, armed with N52 Neodymium magnets."
Sophisticated Minimalism: Less is more. Cut the fluff. Short sentences. Punchy delivery.
Instead of: "You're going to love how this looks on your desk and helps you work better!"
Say: "Workgear styling done right. Elevate your focus."
Sensory & Tactile: Describe how things feel, sound, and move.
Examples: "The satisfying snap," "The smooth glide," "The cold touch of matte aluminum."
The "Insider" Confidence: Speak to the audience as peers who "get it." We don't beg for attention; we present the standard.

Words We Love vs. Words We Ban:
Use: Precision, Crafted, Ecosystem, Tactile, Seamless, Elevate, Haptic, Flow.
Ban: Cheap, Bargain, Hack, Magic (unless referring to tech integration), Cute, Fun.

3. DIGITAL APPEARANCE & VISUAL IDENTITY
The visual aesthetic must reflect the physical products: premium, dark, and perfectly lit.
Color Palette:
Primary: Matte Black (Dominant), Gunmetal Grey, Anodized Silver.
Secondary (Accents): Deep Navy or a muted, sophisticated Olive Green (used sparingly for contrast).
White Space: Crucial. Don't clutter the frame. Let the product breathe.
Typography:
Headlines: Bold, geometric sans-serif (e.g., Montserrat, Inter, or SF Pro). Clean, modern, tech-forward.
Body Copy: Clean, highly legible sans-serif.
Styling: Generous tracking (letter spacing) for a premium feel. Avoid cursive or playful fonts entirely.
Photography & Video Style:
Lighting: Moody, directional lighting. High contrast (chiaroscuro). We want distinct shadows that highlight the metallic textures and machined edges of the products. Avoid flat, bright, evenly lit "stock photo" looks.
Environment: Modern, clean desk setups. Elements can include mechanical keyboards, succulents, dark wood grains, and matte tech accessories.
The "Hero" Shots: Close-up macro photography focusing on details—the hinge of The Trigger, the blade of The Slay, the texture of the NFC card.

4. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC APPEARANCE GUIDELINES
Instagram Grid (The Lookbook)
Aesthetic: Dark, moody, and highly curated.
Format: Alternating between high-res macro product shots, lifestyle "desk flex" setups, and clean graphic carousels (e.g., "5 Ways to Use the SlickTek One").
Rule: No messy backgrounds. Every post must look like it belongs in a premium design magazine.
TikTok & Reels (The ASMR Engine)
Aesthetic: Fast-paced, sensory-heavy.
Format: Focus on the sounds of the product. No voiceover needed for these—let the "snap" and "click" do the talking. High-quality lighting is still mandatory, even for fast-moving video.
Text On-Screen: Keep it minimal. Use native fonts but stick to the brand colors (white/black text with subtle shadows).
LinkedIn (The Professional Front)
Aesthetic: Clean, authoritative, and data-backed.
Format: High-quality document carousels with strong typography. Images should feature the products in professional settings (e.g., networking events, modern offices).

5. COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT TONALITY
How we reply to comments and DMs:
Handling Praise: "Appreciate the support. Welcome to the ecosystem." (Keep it cool and appreciative).
Handling Questions: Direct, factual, and helpful. "The Trigger uses N52 magnets, strong enough to hold an iPad Pro. Let us know if you need the full spec sheet."
Handling Objections (Price): Never apologize for the price. "We engineer with aerospace aluminum, not plastic. It’s an investment in your daily workflow."

1. TOFU (Top of Funnel): Awareness & Traffic Generation
Objective: Drive high-volume, low-cost targeted traffic and establish visual brand equity. 
Target Metrics: CPM, CTR (Click-Through Rate), 3-Second Video View Rate, Cost Per Outbound Click.
The Hook (Meta Ads & TikTok): Lead with sensory, fast-paced creative. Use ASMR-style hooks focusing on the snap of the MagSafe or the smooth mechanism of "The Trigger." Market Bifurcation: US Market: Position around the "Minimalist/MKBHD Desk Aesthetic" and ergonomic wellness.
India Market: Position around premium status, durability (aerospace aluminum), and outsmarting the competition (NFC networking).
Organic SEO & YouTube: Leverage search intent. Optimize YouTube video titles, descriptions, and high-contrast thumbnails around queries like "Best MagSafe Stand for Deep Work" or "Aesthetic Desk Setup 2026."

2. MOFU (Middle of Funnel): Consideration & Micro-Commitments
Objective: Build trust, overcome price objections, and capture data for retargeting. 
Target Metrics: Landing Page View Rate, Email/SMS Opt-in Rate, Retargeting Audience Size.
The Trust Sequence (Retargeting): Serve middle-of-funnel ads to users who watched 50%+ of a TOFU video but didn't click. Shift the messaging from aesthetic to engineering. Use "Founder Story" style videos or deep dives into why N52 Neodymium magnets and CNC machining justify the premium price tag.
B2B Lead Generation (LinkedIn): For the SlickTek One NFC cards, run LinkedIn Lead Gen campaigns targeting HR Directors and Sales VP titles. Offer a "Corporate Gifting Calculator" or a free sample card to capture their email and phone number.
The Opt-In Offer: Instead of a generic "10% off your first order," offer a lead magnet that fits the brand: "Enter your email for our Guide to the Ultimate Ergonomic Desk Setup + Get 10% off your first SlickTek purchase."

3. BOFU (Bottom of Funnel): Conversion & Cart Optimization
Objective: Maximize Average Order Value (AOV) and seamlessly capture the initial sale. 
Target Metrics: Conversion Rate (CVR), Average Order Value (AOV), Cart Abandonment Rate, ROAS.
Strategic Bundling: Never force the user to buy items piecemeal. Create pre-configured bundles like "The Creator Pack" (The Crane + The Trigger) or "The Networker" (Zip-Tap Wallet + SlickTek One Card) with an aggregate discount.
Frictionless Checkout & Upsells: * Include a MagSafe compatibility checker directly on the product page to kill the "will this work for my phone" objection.
Deploy an in-cart order bump: "Add 'The Slay' Unboxing Knife to your order for just ₹399 / $12."
High-Urgency Retargeting: Deploy dynamic product ads (DPA) on Meta and Google PMax for cart abandoners, layered with user-generated content (UGC) testimonials.

4. POST-PURCHASE & ACTIVATION (The SaaS Bridge)
Objective: Transition the physical buyer into an active digital user of the SlickTek ecosystem. 
Target Metrics: SaaS Profile Setup Completion Rate, Time to First "Tap."
The Unboxing Experience: The packaging must include a highly visible, premium QR code that drives the user directly into the SaaS onboarding flow.

Day 1: Welcome & hardware setup guide.
Day 3: How to build your SlickTek One digital profile for maximum impact.
Day 7: Integrating your SlickTek profile with Hubspot/Google Contacts.
Day 14: The "Pro" features you unlock with the Rs. 499/year subscription.

Objective: Drive recurring revenue and turn existing users into organic acquisition channels. 
Target Metrics: Churn Rate (for SaaS), Viral Coefficient (K-factor), Customer Lifetime Value (LTV).
